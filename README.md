## Engineering Philosophy

I am a Software Development Engineer and Researcher focused on the friction points of modern computing: latency, scalability, and resource constraints. I recently graduated with an M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Texas at Arlington, specializing in AI/ML systems and distributed computing.

As a Systems Engineer, I do not just build APIs; I architect fault-tolerant distributed systems. My background includes designing event-driven microservices in Go and Kubernetes that handle massive concurrency (75k+ connections) without buckling under load. I specialize in non-blocking I/O patterns and optimizing data ingestion pipelines using Kafka and ScyllaDB.

As a Researcher, I treat Machine Learning as a systems problem. My work at UT Arlington targets deployment of heavy Deep Reinforcement Learning (DQN) models onto constrained edge devices. By leveraging C++/CUDA for compute graph optimization and INT8 quantization, I build AI that is not just accurate but efficient enough to run in real-time VR environments.
